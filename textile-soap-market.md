<p><p>The textile soap market is segmented into four main types based on ionic properties. Anionic soaps, typically used for their excellent cleaning and foaming capabilities, effectively remove dirt. Cationic soaps, with their positive charge, serve as softeners and antimicrobial agents, enhancing fabric quality. Zwitterionic soaps possess both positive and negative charges, offering mildness and compatibility with various fabrics. Nonionic soaps, with no charge, are versatile, stable across different pH levels, and suitable for delicate textiles, making them popular in modern textile applications.</p></p>
