<p><p>Gas pipeline infrastructure can be categorized into two primary market types: transmission and distribution. The transmission market involves high-capacity pipelines that transport natural gas over long distances from production sites to distribution centers. This phase is essential for moving large volumes efficiently. In contrast, the distribution market consists of smaller pipelines that deliver gas from local distribution points to end-users, including residential, commercial, and industrial consumers. Both markets are crucial for ensuring a reliable gas supply.</p></p>
