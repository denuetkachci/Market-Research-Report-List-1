The Normally Closed type remains shut during normal operations, ensuring safety by preventing the flow of fluids until activated. Conversely, the Normally Open type is designed to allow flow during regular conditions and closes upon activation, often used for immediate safety measures. Both types play critical roles in industrial processes, providing essential control mechanisms in emergencies to prevent hazards and ensure operational safety.</p></p>
